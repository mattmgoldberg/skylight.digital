Why Skylight?

Our mission matters

We’re more than a company. We represent a movement to reinvent how the government serves families, patients, veterans, immigrants, and many others in today’s digital world. Everything about us — our structure, culture, services, work, thoughts, and team — is designed with that purpose in mind.

You matter

No crafty policy or flashy tech is going to change the way government works. It’s people like you, with talents in research, design, product, engineering, data, and more, who are given a chance to make a difference. We’ve created an environment that empowers you to unleash your skills for public good, whether that’s improving early childhood outcomes or helping to save veteran lives. You’ll work alongside a team of extraordinarily-talented people who strive to bring out the best in you and each other. You’ll also be taken care of with a well-thought-out compensation and benefits package. We want you to be happy, fulfilled, and well-supported.

We’re just getting started

We’re still in startup mode, and the future is looking bright. We’re doing impactful work, building an amazing team, growing quickly, and improving every day. Everyone is given the opportunity to shape the company in some way. We’d love for you to join us in creating something special.

Care package

We hire good people and support them with the best compensation and benefits possible. We keep an open dialog with everyone on the team to make what we offer even better.

Compensation

We value you and your contributions. We offer a competitive salary, a stake in the company’s success, performance bonuses, referral bonuses, thought leadership bonuses (for example, blog posts), a quick-pay program, and HUBZone resident incentives (for example, a monthly housing stipend).

Future

We want to see you grow. We match your 401(k) at 10% of your salary. We also offer an accrued budget of up to $2,000 per year for professional development, which includes conferences, training, travel, and materials.

Health

You and your family’s health are important to us. We cover 90% of your medical premium, 100% premiums for vision and dental, and 50% premiums for dependents. We also provide life insurance, short- and long-term disability insurance, AD&D insurance, healthcare and dependent care flexible spending accounts, and health savings account.

Parental leave

Some moments you can never get back. We offer a minimum of X weeks paid off for all eligible new birth, adoption, or foster parents.

Time off

We want you to enjoy life outside of work. We offer an unlimited paid time-off policy, which covers any type of leave (such as sick and vacation) that you need or want to take.

Flexibility

We trust you. We give you maximum flexibility to decide when, where, and how to get your work done. We're a distributed and remote-friendly team, currently spread out across 17 different states. How do we make that work? Check out this guide.

Tools

Good work requires good tools. We provide an annual budget of up to $750 for anything, such as a new computer, that you think will help to do your job better. Most software that you need will be provided by us.

Code of conduct

Everyone deserves a safe, inclusive environment. We live by our Code of Conduct, and actively look for ways to make it even stronger, as well as reinforce expected behaviors.

Community

Amplify your impact. We give you pathways to engage with a growing community of organizations and individuals working to change how the government works, such as the Digital Services Coalition, which we co-founded.

Interview process

If you apply for a position, here’s what you can expect in terms of the process:

1. Application review

After you submit your application, we'll evaluate your information against the qualifications of our open positions and communicate next steps. (We encourage you to include links to samples of your work, if you have any.) If you look like a potential match, we'll begin the live interview process.

2. Preliminary screening

The interview process begins with a 15-minute or 30-minute preliminary screening call by phone or video. Plan to talk about your skills and experience, what you're passionate about, and the work we do here. The call will also cover the interview process, structure, basic employment requirements, and employee benefits. If the preliminary screening call goes well, we'll schedule you for a skills interview and a behavioral interview. Both interviews are generally held by phone or video.

3. Skills interview

The skills interview lasts approximately one and a half hours. You'll be asked a variety of questions about your experience and skills, and how you'd apply them within the context of Skylight's work.

4. Behavioral interview

The behavioral interview lasts approximately one hour, and will assess how you interact with colleagues and get work done.

5. Additional interviews (if needed)

In some cases, we'll conduct additional interviews to evaluate areas we’d like to know more about.

6. The offer

If all the interviews go well, you'll receive a preliminary offer followed by an official offer, including an agreed-upon start date!
